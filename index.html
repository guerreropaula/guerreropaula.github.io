01. About

I'm a Spanish-born NLP researcher and computational linguist with a rare double background: a degree in Translation & Interpreting (English, French, Spanish, Chinese) combined with advanced training in deep learning and natural language processing. Currently pursuing my Master's in Natural Language Analysis and Processing at the University of the Basque Country (UPV/EHU), I focus on low-resource machine translation, LLM fine-tuning, and multilingual corpora.

My work sits at the intersection of linguistics and AI — I'm passionate about bringing underrepresented languages into modern NLP systems, with a particular focus on Valencian and other regional varieties. I also care about making AI accessible: my clinical text simplification project aims to bridge the gap between medical jargon and patient understanding.

Outside the lab, I've lived and studied in Brussels (Erasmus), worked at a water park in New York State, and competed in dual-qualification French–Spanish high school exams with near-perfect marks. Multilingualism is not just my field — it's my life.

04. Projects

📄 EAMT 2026 Submission

Enhancing LLM Translation Performance for Spanish–Valencian through SFT and Reinforcement Learning

University of the Basque Country · Feb 2026 – Present

Fine-tuned TranslateGemma-4B using Supervised Fine-Tuning (SFT) and Group Relative Policy Optimization (GRPO) to improve translation quality for the low-resource Spanish→Valencian direction. Designed custom reward functions and an end-to-end evaluation pipeline. Results: +22.9 BLEU and +15.7 chrF over baseline; Valencian-specific dialect forms grew from 3.2% to 41.0%, approximating professional human translation quality.

Clinical Text Simplification with Fine-Tuned LLMs

Feb 2026 – Present

Fine-tuned Qwen3.5-0.8B and Llama-3.2-1B with LoRA on the CLARA-MeD clinical corpus for automatic medical text simplification in Spanish. Achieved results comparable to human simplifications in meaning preservation. Deployed interactive demos on Hugging Face.
